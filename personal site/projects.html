my projects

NYT Visual

NYT Visual is an iOS experiment with one goal in mind: to engage with Gen-Z readers using a visual-first landing page as a gateway into the news.

In an age when most of my peers scroll through content and spend only a few seconds on an image unless it catches our interest, we believe the text-first news layout may be overwhelming and less engaging. I worked on frontend (Swift) and backend (Python) alongside other interns and full-time employees during Maker Week, The Times's week-long summer hackathon. We then presented our prototype at a company-wide event.

Key takeaways:

-Working cross-functionally with data, software, design, and product.

-Developing a mission & vision with a specific age-group in mind.

-Setting a timeline for iOS and backend delivery.

-Working with Firebase.

Crumble

Crumble is a cooking app which allows readers to catalog their ingredients and find popular recipes they can make, using just what's in their fridge.

I worked on iOS frontend for the search and home pages for Crumble alongside 3 fellow students: 1 iOS/backend, 1 backend, and 1 product designer. We were given one week to formulate an idea, design, and mobile prototype to be judged by other students in a challenge sponsored by Airbnb, Amazon, Bose, Facebook, Google, and Microsoft.

AppDev Hack Challenge 2019

Key takeaways:

-First experience being "scrappy" during a hackathon.

-Prioritizing the home and search pages as an MVP and then iterating.

-Winner of Cornell AppDev's Hachathon (Spring 2019).

NYT Offers Dashboard

I built a product metrics dashboard for The New York Times subscription offers, such as "Starting at $1/week for 1 year". I created visualizations for overall traffic based on time, price tier, and country, as well as comparisons of the offers themselves. [Confidential information has been hidden in the video.]

I was asked to conduct a workshop open to the entire company during Maker Week on how to create a clear, comprehensible, and aesthetic dashboard for a product-oriented team.

Key takeaways:

-Learning how to present information differently to stakeholders vs engineers.

Carriage

Carriage is an iOS and Android app which aims to connect students with disabilities at Cornell to daily transportation services in a clear, streamlined way.

This is a work-in-progress application created by Cornell Design & Tech Initiative, a student-led, cross-functional app development team. As a student with a disability, this project is very close to my heart.

Key takeaways:

-Implementing an app with accessibility standards (level AAA).

-Learning how to build a codebase from scratch.

-Working with Google Authentication and SSO.
